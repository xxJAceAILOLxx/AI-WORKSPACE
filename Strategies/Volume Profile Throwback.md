# Strategy: The Throwback

## Thesis
After a violent high-volume break out of value, the first retest is the highest-probability trade. Trapped traders cover into it.

## Requirements

- RTH (regular trading hours) profile only
- Short-term profile (current session or prior session)
- Must have clear value area with tight overlap

---

![[Images/Throwback Setup.svg]]

## Entry Criteria

1. **Clean value area** — tight, confined, well-defined VAH/VAL
2. **Violent break** — high-volume move cleanly outside value (1.5x+ average candle size)
3. **First retest** — price returns to the edge of value (VAH or VAL) within 2 sessions
4. **Flow confirmation** — on retest, momentum stalls, absorption shows (buying if shorting, selling if buying)

## Entry
- **Direction**: Favor the break direction. If break was down → short on retest to VAL.
- **Trigger**: Price touches VAH/VAL and flow shows rejection (e.g. a bid sweep that can't hold, or an offer stack that doesn't lift)
- **Execution**: Market order on confirmation

## Stop
- **Placement**: Beyond the rejection wick by 1 ATR (or 2x the average candle range of the session)
- **If stopped**: Level is not holding. Re-evaluate. Could be false break or context shift.

## Target
- **TP1**: Break even (1R)
- **TP2**: Extensions: 1x value range width beyond the break (move the same distance value was wide)
- **Trail**: If TP2 hits, trail by 1 ATR below/above

---

## Do NOT Take

- Retest after 3+ sessions — positioning has decayed
- Break on low volume — not real conviction
- Market in overlapping composites — too messy to define value
- Third+ retest of same level — pressure building, may snap the other way

## Ideal Conditions
- After-hours session led to gap / head fake → RTH retest of value edge
- News catalyst → high-volume break → retest as noise clears

# Reflection: Volume Profile

## What I Learned

Volume profile is simpler than most people make it. It's just a histogram of where business occurred. The moment you start reading "support" or "resistance" into it, you're making assumptions.

## Biggest Mindset Shift

> "The shorter and cleaner it is, the more you could tell about live positioning."

This reframes everything. I used to think older levels held more weight because they were "bigger." Wrong. They're noisier. Time decays positioning relevance fast.

## What I'll Stop Doing

- Using old monthly POCs as key levels
- Treating every HVN as support/resistance
- Ignoring what regime those profiles came from

## What I'll Start Doing

- Asking "is this level still fresh?" before trading it
- Separating context from execution
- Letting short-term profile structure lead, flow confirm

## What Surprised Me

The idea that repeated tests degrade the level rather than reinforce it. Intuitively I'd think "tested 5 times = strong level." The video argues the opposite — pressure builds, resolution gets more violent. Makes sense.

> "Early tests are information. Repeated tests... buildup of pressure."

## Open Questions

- How do I systematically measure "freshness" of positioning? Tick charts? Session-based?
- When does a level transition from actionable positioning to just context? Specific time decay model?

# The Proximity Principle

## Definition
The closer in time to when turnover took place, the more likely that positioning is still relevant and actionable.

---

## Why

- Traders who entered recently still hold those positions
- They care about price returning to their entry
- They will likely react (cover, add, panic) on retest
- Over time, positions get turned over, closed, or become irrelevant

## Decay Chain

| Time Since Turnover | Likelihood of Live Positioning |
|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| Minutes ago | Very high |
| 1 session ago | High |
| 1 week ago | Moderate |
| 1 month ago | Low |
| Multiple months | Very low — context only |

## Nuance

> "It's not to say that there isn't positioning, it's just likely that we're running into participants that don't really give a [damn] about price trading a few ticks back towards their entry."

Longer-term positions are:
- Spread over a range of prices
- Already turned over multiple times
- Held by participants less reactive to short-term retests

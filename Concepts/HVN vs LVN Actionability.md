# HVN vs LVN Actionability

## High Volume Nodes (HVN)
- Market paused — two-way trade
- Where traders likely built positions
- Expect reactions FROM these levels — they act as magnets/mirrors

## Low Volume Nodes (LVN)
- Market zipped through — rejected or trapped
- Where stops were likely hit
- Expect reactions THROUGH these levels — they act as speed bumps

## Why LVNs Are Actionable

> "The market can zip through them again for a variety of reasons. What we can say is, 'The market was less accepted there.'"

On a return to an LVN:
- Anyone who got positioned at the nearby HVN may want to exit at breakeven
- Stops were previously run here — soft stops likely remain
- "People that are in position probably like to bail out closer to their position"

## Proximity Rule
Same as everything else — [[The Proximity Principle]] applies.

> "An LVN from the prior session matters a lot more than an LVN from 6 months ago."

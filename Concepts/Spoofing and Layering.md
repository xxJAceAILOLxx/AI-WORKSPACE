# Spoofing and Layering

## One-Line Summary
Manipulation patterns where fake orders create illusion of supply/demand to mislead other participants.

---

## Spoofing

### What It Is
- Place large order with **no intent to execute**
- Cancel before fill after others react to it
- Creates illusion of supply or demand
- Regulatory offense in most markets

### How to Spot
- Large order appears, price moves toward it
- Order gets cancelled just before price reaches it
- Pattern repeats at different levels
- "Painting the tape" — showing interest that isn't real

### Why It Works (Against You)
- Retail sees large bid → thinks support → goes long
- Spoof placed below price → price moves up → spoof cancelled
- Retail is now long at the high → price drops

---

## Layering

### What It Is
- Multiple fake orders at **different price levels**
- Creates illusion of depth on one side of the book
- Orders disappear when approached

### How to Spot
- Many orders stacked at several levels
- As price approaches, orders get pulled simultaneously
- Book looks thick but fills thin
- "Paper wall" — looks strong, dissolves on contact

---

## How to Protect Yourself

| Red Flag | What It Means |
|----------|--------------|
| Large order appears and disappears quickly | Likely spoof |
| Orders just below/above price that never fill | Creating illusion |
| Book looks thick but fills are thin | Layering |
| Large order at psychological level (round number) | Common spoof location |

### Survival Rules
1. **Don't trust large resting orders at face value**
2. **Watch if they actually fill or just disappear**
3. **Combine with absorption analysis** — real interest absorbs, fake interest retreats
4. **Use flow confirmation** — delta shows if orders are genuine

---

## Why It Matters for Volume Profile

- Spoofing can create false support/resistance at profile levels
- A POC or VAH/VAL with spoofed orders won't hold
- Real absorption vs spoofed interest = the difference between a working setup and a trap

---

## Regulatory Context

- Spoofing is illegal in most jurisdictions (Dodd-Frank, MiFID II)
- But still happens — especially in crypto (less regulated)
- Detection is getting better but not perfect
- As a trader, you must learn to spot it yourself

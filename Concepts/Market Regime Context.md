# Market Regime Context

## Why Regime Matters for Volume Profile

Profiles covering multiple regimes are less reliable. Different conditions change the meaning of volume.

## Factors That Change

- **Volatility backdrop** — high vol vs low vol changes position sizing
- **Liquidity conditions** — bid/offer spreads, market depth
- **Contract rolls & expiries** — volume from closing vs opening positions
- **Rate cycles** — hiking vs cutting changes environment entirely
- **FOMC chair / policy** — different market expectations

> "Do we want to make an assessment about what took place during a cutting cycle and then a hiking cycle if we have profiles that cover both?"

## Implication
Old profiles are noisy because they aggregate volume from fundamentally different market regimes. This is another reason [[The Proximity Principle]] matters beyond just time.

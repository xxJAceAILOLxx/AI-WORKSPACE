# The Throwback (Post-Breakout Retest)

## Definition
After a convincing high-volume breakout from value, the market retests the breakout level. This retest is the highest-probability trade setup.

## Why It Works

1. Range was tight with lots of overlap → lots of positioning confined
2. Convincing break puts one side offside
3. On retest, trapped participants:
   - Cover their losing positions
   - Are unlikely to re-add
4. The level matters because **people got involved there**, not because it's a line on a chart

> "This level matters not because it's a line on the chart or tool or indicator. It matters because we know where people got involved and where with conviction it's more likely than not that they would be uncomfortable."

## The Mental Model

> "If you were longing up here, you come back the next day, market opens, and it gaps against you a bunch of handles. Are you likely to hold out or are you more likely looking to get out around break even?"

## Relationship to Head Fakes
- Head fake: breakout fails → fade the breakout
- Throwback: breakout succeeds → trade the retest

# Absorption

## One-Line Summary
When large orders absorb selling or buying pressure without letting price through — the level is defended.

---

## What It Is

- Heavy selling absorbed by equal or greater buying → floor
- Heavy buying absorbed by equal or greater selling → ceiling
- Level holds despite repeated attacks = strong positioning
- Break after absorption = trapped participants forced to cover

> "Absorption at a level = don't trade against it."

---

## How It Looks

### Bullish Absorption (Support)
- Price hits a level with large sell orders (aggressive selling)
- Bids absorb everything thrown at them
- Price can't push lower despite heavy volume
- Delta shows: negative prints, but price holds → bids absorbing

### Bearish Absorption (Resistance)
- Price hits a level with large buy orders (aggressive buying)
- Offers absorb everything thrown at them
- Price can't push higher despite heavy volume
- Delta shows: positive prints, but price holds → offers absorbing

---

## Key Signals

| Signal | What It Means |
|--------|--------------|
| High volume + price holds | Absorption in progress |
| Repeated attacks that fail | Level is strongly defended |
| Absorption + volume decline | Sellers/buyers exhausting |
| Absorption break | Trapped participants must cover → violent move |

---

## Integration with Volume Profile

| Setup | Absorption Role |
|-------|----------------|
| Fading Edges of Balance | Absorption at VAH/VAL → confirmation to fade |
| Head Fake | Absorption at swept level → confirms it's a fake |
| Throwback | Absorption on retest → confirmation to enter |

---

## Why It Matters

- Absorption is the strongest confirmation signal in order flow
- Without absorption, a level may break
- With absorption, the level is defended — higher probability setup
- Combines structure (where) with flow (conviction)
